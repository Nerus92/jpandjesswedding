Explore the Venue

Château de la Napoule

In the early 20th century, Henry and Marie Clews stumbled upon the weathered remains of a medieval fortress on the French Riviera. To most, it might have been little more than a forgotten relic of stone and ivy, but to Henry and Marie, it was the beginning of a story—a story of revival, of art, of love. They saw in its broken walls the potential for something timeless, something that could honor the past while creating beauty for the future. Together, they poured their hearts into rebuilding the Château, each decision guided by a shared belief in the transformative power of imagination.

Today, the Château de la Napoule is a testament to their devotion. Its lush gardens, intricate sculptures, and timeless halls breathe with their vision. As an Art Foundation, it now nurtures creativity, inviting artists to draw from its magic while inspiring young minds through programs that take art into schools and communities. There’s an undeniable sense of something larger at play here—something that connects every person who walks through its gates to an ever-widening circle of beauty and inspiration.

Perhaps that’s why this place feels so right for us. It doesn’t simply hold history; it holds wonder. There’s a quiet parallel between the Clews’ vision and the vastness of the galaxy that draws us in. Just as the stars are bound together in constellations, each shaping the story of the night sky, the Château reminds us of the invisible threads connecting us all—threads of creativity, curiosity, and a shared journey.

Perched above the endless blue of the Mediterranean, Château de la Napoule feels like a bridge between worlds: past and future, seen and unseen. It’s more than our wedding venue; it’s a place that mirrors the extraordinary chance of our own story. Here, surrounded by timeless beauty and infinite possibility, we will celebrate the miracle of this moment—of two paths converging, under the same stars.

Travel / Directions

The Château is located 7 kilometres (4 miles) west of Cannes (towards Aix-en-Provence).

By train:

TER to La Napoule stop (situated directly facing the Château) or TGV stopping at Cannes Train Station.

By car:

Highway A8, taking exit 40 to "Mandelieu Centre."

Coming from Italy: the exit is after "Cannes" and "La Bocca"

Coming from Aix/Marseille: the exit is after "Frejus" and "Les Adrets."

Parking:

The château does not have any allocated parking spots.

Here are some nearby parking areas:

  • Parking St Fainéant: 50 places
  • Parking Soustelle: 40 places
  • Some parking spots may be available on Avenue Henry Clews

By plane:

Nice-Côte d'Azur Airport (30 minutes on the highway to Mandelieu La Napoule

Coming from the US?

Option 1: Fly into Nice

  • Fly into Nice (NCE)
  • Grab a rental car and head to the Chateau or your nearby accommodation

Option 2: Fly into Paris (CDG)

  • Fly into Paris (CDG)
  • Take the RER B train to Paris Gare du Nord, then transfer to Paris Gare de Lyon using RER D or Metro line 4
  • Book a high-speed TGV train from Paris Gare de Lyon to Cannes. The journey typically takes around 5 to 6 hours (but is beautiful)
  • Grab a rental car and head to the Château or your nearby accommodation

Option 3: Fly into Paris (ORY)

  • Fly into Paris (ORY)
  • Take the Orlyval train to Antony, then transfer to RER B to Paris Gare du Nord, and proceed to Gare de Lyon.
  • Book a high-speed TGV train from Paris Gare de Lyon to Cannes. The journey typically takes around 5 to 6 hours (but is beautiful)
  • Grab a rental car and head to the Château or your nearby accommodation
